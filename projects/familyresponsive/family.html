  • Music is banned in the Rivera family due to Miguel's great-great-grandfather abandoning his family for music.
  • The director of Coco movie researched Día de los Muertos traditions by visiting real Mexican families.
  • "Remember Me" from Coco won an Academy Award for Best Original Song, becoming an iconic anthem.

Héctor

Héctor is a friendly musician who is stuck in the Land of the Dead. He helps Miguel find his family, and Miguel promises to make his daughter remember him. Héctor loves his family very much and wants to be remembered by them.

Miguel Rivera

Miguel is a young boy who loves music, even though his family does not allow it. He goes to the Land of the Dead to find his great-great-grandfather and learn about his family’s past. Through his journey, Miguel learns the importance of family and following his dreams.

Mama Imelda

Mama Imelda is Miguel’s great-great-grandmother. She stopped music in the family after her husband left and never came back. Later, she learns that family is more important than staying angry and decides to forgive.

Ernesto de la Cruz

Ernesto is a famous singer and Miguel’s hero. Miguel later discovers that Ernesto took someone else’s music and caused a person’s death. His story teaches that fame built on lies is not true success.

Mama Coco

Mama Coco is Miguel's great-grandmother, who holds precious memories of her father, Héctor, despite struggling with age and memory loss. She represents the power of love and memory in the family, especially when Miguel helps her recall her father’s songs. By the end of the story, Mama Coco plays a crucial role in reconnecting her family with its past and honoring Héctor's legacy.
